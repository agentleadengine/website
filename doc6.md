SECTION 1: GETTING STARTED WITH THE CRM

What is the Agent Lead Engine CRM?

The Agent Lead Engine CRM is our customer relationship management and
marketing automation platform. It is the central hub where we manage all
our leads, clients, appointments, communications, and workflows across
five sub-accounts representing different insurance lines.

How to Log In

To access the CRM:

- Navigate to your CRM login URL in your browser

- Enter your username and password

- You will be directed to your default sub-account\'s dashboard

Understanding the Sidebar Navigation

The left sidebar contains the main navigation menu. Familiarize yourself
with these sections:

- Dashboard: overview of key metrics and widgets for the current
  sub-account

- Contacts: your database of all leads and clients

- Opportunities: the pipeline view of deals in progress

- Calendars: appointment booking and scheduling

- Automation: workflows and automated sequences

- Sites: landing pages and funnels

- Settings: configuration for the sub-account

Switching Between Sub-Accounts

Agent Lead Engine has five sub-accounts: Personal Lines, Commercial
Lines, Life Insurance, Medicare & ACA, and Final Expense. To switch
between them:

- Go to the sub-account list in your CRM dashboard

- Search for the sub-account by name

- Click the \'Switch to Sub-Account\' button next to it

IMPORTANT: Do NOT use the sidebar dropdown to switch sub-accounts. It is
unreliable and may cause UI issues.

Window Size Requirement

Your browser window must be at least 1280px wide at all times. Narrower
windows can cause UI elements to misalign or hide, making it difficult
to work effectively. You can check your window width in most browsers by
opening Developer Tools (F12) and checking the viewport dimensions.

SECTION 2: THE DASHBOARD

What the Dashboard Shows

The dashboard is your at-a-glance overview of the current sub-account\'s
performance. It displays key metrics, recent activity, and data widgets
that help you monitor leads and deals.

Standard Widgets

Every sub-account has these standard widgets:

- New Leads This Month: count of new leads that arrived this month

- Closed Won This Month: count of deals successfully closed

- Revenue This Month: total revenue generated this month

- Closed Lost This Month: count of deals lost to competitors or no-shows

- Pipeline Overview: a donut chart showing the breakdown of deal stages

- Active Policies: count of currently active policies

- Stale Leads: count of leads that haven\'t progressed in 14+ days

Sub-Account-Specific Widgets

Depending on the sub-account, you may see additional widgets:

- Discovery Calls: count of scheduled discovery calls

- Underwriting In Progress: count of applications under review

- Applications In Progress: count of pending applications

- Enrollments: count of completed enrollments

- Turning 65 Leads: count of Medicare-eligible prospects (Medicare & ACA
  only)

- Approvals vs Declines: ratio of approved to declined applications

Reading the Widgets

Each widget displays a number and sometimes a trend indicator (up, down,
or neutral). The numbers represent actual counts or dollar amounts.
Understanding what each number means helps you quickly assess the health
of the business and identify areas that need attention.

Real-Time Data Refresh

CRM dashboards update in real time as new leads come in, deals
are closed, and workflows execute. You do not need to manually refresh
the page, though you may do so with Ctrl+R or Cmd+R if you want to see
the very latest data immediately.

SECTION 3: CONTACTS

How to Find a Contact

There are two ways to find a contact in the CRM:

- Use the search bar at the top of the Contacts section and type the
  contact\'s name, phone number, or email

- Browse the Contacts list to scroll through all contacts in the current
  sub-account

Contact Record Overview

Each contact record contains the following information:

- Name: the contact\'s full name

- Phone: primary phone number

- Email: primary email address

- Tags: labels applied to organize and automate follow-ups

- Notes: internal notes about interactions, preferences, or status

- Activity Timeline: chronological log of all interactions (calls, SMS,
  emails, form submissions, notes)

How to Add a Note to a Contact

To document an interaction or important information:

- Open the contact record

- Scroll to the Notes section

- Click \'Add Note\' and type your message

- Click Save

Notes are visible to all team members and appear in the activity
timeline.

How to Add or Remove a Tag

Tags help organize contacts and trigger automations:

- Open the contact record

- Find the Tags section

- To add a tag: type the tag name in the input field and click Save

- To remove a tag: click the X next to the tag name

WARNING: Adding or removing tags can trigger workflows. Be intentional
and consult your manager if unsure.

How to View Pipeline Stage

To see what stage a contact\'s deal is in:

- Open the contact record

- Look for the Pipeline or Opportunities section, which shows the
  associated deal and its current stage (e.g., \'New Lead\', \'Needs
  Analysis\', \'Ready to Buy\', \'Closed Won\')

How to Make a Call or Send an SMS

From the contact record:

- To call: click the phone icon next to the phone number (requires VoIP
  integration)

- To send SMS: click the message icon next to the phone number and type
  your message

Important: Never Delete a Contact Without Approval

Deleting a contact removes all associated history and cannot be undone
easily. Always ask your manager for approval before deleting any
contact.

SECTION 4: OPPORTUNITIES (THE PIPELINE)

What is an Opportunity?

An opportunity is a deal in progress. It represents a potential sale and
tracks its movement through various stages from initial contact to
closing. Each opportunity is linked to a contact and shows the deal
value, stage, and history.

How to View the Pipeline

To see the pipeline view:

- Click \'Opportunities\' in the sidebar

- Select \'Pipeline view\' to see deals organized by stage in vertical
  columns

Understanding Pipeline Stages

The general flow of a deal moves through these stages:

- New Lead: prospect just entered the system

- Initial Contact / Needs Analysis: gathering information about the
  prospect\'s needs

- Quote Provided: you have sent a proposal or quote

- Ready to Buy: prospect is showing strong buying signals and is ready
  to move forward

- Application / Underwriting: formal application is submitted or being
  reviewed

- Closed Won: deal is successfully closed and policy is issued

- Closed Lost: prospect decided not to buy or chose a competitor

Sub-accounts may have slightly different stage names based on their line
of business.

How to Move a Deal to a Different Stage

There are two methods:

- Drag and drop the deal card from one stage column to another

- Click the deal card, open details, and select a new stage from the
  dropdown

When you move a deal, workflows may trigger automatically (for example,
a pipeline stage alert or follow-up sequence).

What Happens When You Move a Deal

Moving a deal to a new stage can trigger automations:

- A workflow may send an internal alert to the agent

- A follow-up sequence may begin automatically

- Tags may be added or removed based on stage rules

Always move deals intentionally and understand the consequences.

How to Create a New Opportunity Manually

If a deal was not automatically created by a form submission:

- Go to Opportunities section

- Click \'Create New Opportunity\' or a similar button

- Fill in the deal name, associated contact, deal value, and stage

- Click Save

How to Update Opportunity Details

To modify a deal:

- Click the deal card to open its details

- Update the name, value, source, or any other field

- Click Save

Closed Won vs Closed Lost

Closed Won: the prospect bought the policy. These deals contribute to
revenue and are typically archived.

Closed Lost: the prospect did not buy. Reasons may include choosing a
competitor, missing application deadlines, or losing contact. These
deals are also archived but help you identify why leads are lost.

What to Do With Stale Leads

Stale leads are deals that haven\'t progressed for 14 or more days. The
\'Deal Pulse\' workflow automatically tags stale leads with
\'needs-follow-up\'. When you see a stale lead:

- Review the activity timeline to understand where the deal is stuck

- Add a note about your findings

- Reach out to the agent to determine next steps

- Update the stage or add a note about the reason for delay

SECTION 5: WORKFLOWS AND AUTOMATIONS

What are Workflows?

Workflows are automated sequences that trigger based on events. For
example, when a new lead comes in, a workflow can automatically send
them an introductory email and SMS, add a tag, or create a calendar
invitation. Workflows save time and ensure consistent follow-up.

Your Role with Workflows

You do NOT build workflows. That is an admin task. Your job is to
understand what workflows do, monitor whether they fire correctly, and
report any issues.

Key Workflows in Every Sub-Account

Speed-to-Lead / New Lead Auto-Reply

Fires when a new lead comes in. Typically sends an immediate SMS or
email thanking them for their interest and setting expectations for
follow-up.

Form Submission - Tag & Pipeline

When someone fills out a funnel form, this workflow creates an
opportunity in the pipeline and applies initial tags.

Missed Call Text-Back

If the agent misses an incoming call, this workflow automatically sends
an SMS to the lead acknowledging the missed call and requesting a
callback.

Referral Request

After a deal closes (Closed Won), this workflow asks the client for
referrals, encouraging word-of-mouth growth.

Renewal Reminder

Reminds clients before their policy renewal date, encouraging them to
renew early or upgrade.

Deal Pulse

Monitors deals for lack of activity. If a deal hasn\'t progressed in 14+
days, Deal Pulse automatically tags it \'needs-follow-up\' and may alert
the team.

How to Check if a Workflow Fired

After an event occurs (e.g., a form submission or tag application), you
can verify the workflow executed:

- Open the contact\'s record

- Scroll to the activity timeline

- Look for entries like \'Tag added: new-lead\' or \'Email sent: New
  Lead Follow-Up\'

If you see these entries, the workflow fired successfully.

What to Do if a Workflow Did NOT Fire

If you expect a workflow to fire but it doesn\'t:

- Do NOT attempt to fix or edit the workflow yourself

- Notify your manager immediately with details: contact name, expected
  workflow, timestamp

- Your manager can investigate and correct the workflow configuration

CRITICAL: Never Toggle Workflows

Workflows can be toggled between Draft (off) and Published (on) states.
NEVER toggle a workflow on or off unless your manager explicitly
instructs you to do so. Toggling workflows can break automations and
cause critical processes to fail.

SECTION 6: CALENDARS AND APPOINTMENTS

Where to Find Calendars

To access calendar settings and view appointments:

- Go to Settings in the sidebar

- Look for Calendar Settings or Calendars option

The Five Sub-Account Calendars

Each sub-account has one booking calendar with a specific name and
duration:

- Personal Lines: Free Quote Consultation (15 minutes)

- Commercial Lines: Discovery Call (30 minutes)

- Life Insurance: Needs Analysis Call (30 minutes)

- Medicare & ACA: Plan Comparison Call (30 minutes)

- Final Expense: Coverage Discussion (20 minutes)

How to View Upcoming Appointments

From the calendar view:

- You can see appointments scheduled for today, this week, or this month
  depending on the view

- Click on an appointment to see details: client name, time, duration,
  and notes

How to Reschedule or Cancel an Appointment

To modify an appointment:

- Click on the appointment

- Select \'Reschedule\' or \'Cancel\'

- If rescheduling, select a new time and confirm

- If canceling, confirm and optionally add a reason

The system typically sends notifications to the client when appointments
are changed.

The Booking URL

Each calendar has a unique URL that clients use to book appointments.
You may share this URL in emails, text messages, or your website.
Clients visit the URL, see available time slots, and book directly.
Common URL format: your CRM booking URL / calendar ID

What Happens When Someone Books

When a prospect books an appointment through the calendar:

- The appointment is automatically added to the calendar

- The \'Calendar Booking Alert\' workflow fires

- An email notification is sent to the team

- If the contact is new, a new contact record may be created

SECTION 7: FUNNELS AND FORMS

What is a Funnel?

A funnel is a landing page where prospects submit their information to
request more details, book a consultation, or apply for a policy. Each
sub-account has one primary funnel.

Funnel Structure

Typical funnel structure:

- Landing page: describes the product or service and includes an intake
  form

- Thank you page: displayed after form submission, thanks the prospect
  and sets expectations

How Leads Flow In

The funnel lead generation process works like this:

- Prospect visits the landing page URL

- Prospect fills out the form with their name, email, phone, and line of
  business

- Prospect is redirected to the thank you page

- The \'Form Submission - Tag & Pipeline\' workflow creates a new
  contact and opportunity

- The new lead appears in the Contacts and Opportunities sections

Intake Forms vs Funnel Forms

Intake forms and funnel forms are different:

- Funnel forms: public-facing, used by prospects to submit leads from
  external sources

- Intake forms: internal, used by agents or VAs to manually enter lead
  details when a prospect calls or emails

How to Submit a Lead Using the Intake Form

If a prospect provides information via phone, email, or other channel
(not through the funnel):

- Go to the Contacts section

- Click \'Add Contact\' or find an intake form option

- Fill in the prospect\'s name, phone, email, and any relevant details

- Apply appropriate tags (e.g., \'referral\' if they were referred,
  \'phone-lead\' if they called)

- Click Save

This creates a new contact record and may trigger relevant workflows.

IMPORTANT: Never Edit Funnel Pages or Forms

Funnel pages and forms are configured by managers or admins. Editing
them can break lead capture, change the customer experience, or cause
workflows to fail. Never edit a funnel page or form without explicit
manager approval.

SECTION 8: TAGS AND THEIR MEANINGS

What are Tags?

Tags are labels applied to contacts that serve two purposes: organizing
your database and triggering workflows. When you add a tag to a contact,
specific automations may fire in response.

Frequently Used Tags

You will encounter these tags regularly:

- new-lead: just entered the system, not yet contacted

- policy-active: has an active, in-force policy with the agency

- ready-to-buy: showing strong buying signals, likely to close soon
  (applied by Deal Pulse or agent judgment)

- needs-follow-up: has gone quiet and requires outreach (applied by Deal
  Pulse when a deal is stale)

- referral: was referred by an existing customer

Line-Specific Tags

Tags also identify the type of insurance or product:

Property & Casualty

- auto: auto insurance

- home: homeowners insurance

- renters: renters insurance

- bop: business owners policy

- workers-comp: workers compensation insurance

Life Insurance

- term: term life insurance

- whole-life: permanent whole life insurance

Health & Medicare

- medicare-advantage: Medicare Advantage plan

- turning-65: prospect turning 65 and eligible for Medicare

- guaranteed-issue: guaranteed issue coverage

- simplified-issue: simplified underwriting process

How to Apply a Tag Manually

To add a tag to a contact:

- Open the contact record

- Find the Tags section

- Type the tag name (e.g., \'ready-to-buy\') in the input field

- Click Save

How to Remove a Tag

To remove a tag from a contact:

- Open the contact record

- Find the Tags section

- Click the X next to the tag name

WARNING: Tags Trigger Workflows

Adding or removing tags can trigger workflows. Be careful and
intentional when tagging. For example, adding \'ready-to-buy\' might
trigger a sales acceleration sequence. If you are unsure whether a tag
will cause an unwanted automation, ask your manager before applying it.

SECTION 9: DAILY ROUTINES FOR VAs

Morning Routine

Start your day by reviewing the system and getting up to speed:

- Log in to the CRM

- Check the dashboard for each sub-account you manage (review new leads,
  revenue, pipeline health)

- Review new leads that came in overnight (click Contacts and filter by
  date added)

- Check for upcoming appointments today (review the Calendar)

- Review any internal notification emails from workflows or your manager

Throughout the Day

Maintain the CRM and support your agents:

- Monitor incoming leads and verify that automations fired correctly

- Update pipeline stages as agents report progress on deals

- Add notes to contact records after agent calls or interactions

- Respond to client support requests or questions

- Submit intake forms for leads that come in via phone or other
  non-funnel channels

End of Day Routine

Prepare for the next day and ensure the system is healthy:

- Review the pipeline for any leads that need follow-up tomorrow (stale
  leads, pending quotes)

- Verify that no workflows are stuck (check for contacts without
  expected tags or notifications)

- Document any issues you encountered and prepare to report them to
  management

SECTION 10: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

Learn from these common pitfalls to protect the integrity of the CRM:

- Accidentally deleting a contact. Deletions cannot be undone easily.
  Always ask for approval before deleting.

- Moving a deal to the wrong stage. This triggers workflows. Always
  double-check the stage before moving.

- Adding the wrong tag. Tags trigger workflows. Be intentional and
  verify the tag name before saving.

- Editing a workflow. Workflows are built and maintained by admins. Do
  not edit them. Report issues instead.

- Editing a funnel page. Funnels are configured by managers. Do not edit
  them without approval.

- Using the sidebar dropdown to switch sub-accounts. It is unreliable.
  Always use the location list URL instead.

- Working in a window narrower than 1280px. This causes UI bugs and
  misaligned elements. Always keep your window wide.

- Clicking \'Cancel\' or \'Discard\' on unsaved changes dialogs. This
  can wipe your work. Review what you are about to discard.

- Double-clicking action nodes in workflows. This resets them. Click
  once and wait for the system to respond.

- Forgetting to save before navigating away. Always save changes before
  leaving a record or page.
