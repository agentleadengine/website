SECTION 1: OVERVIEW

Purpose

Ensure a professional, consistent process when a client cancels their
service. The goal is to leave every departing client with a positive
impression. They may return or refer others.

Our Policies

- Month-to-month service with no long-term contracts

- No cancellation fees

- 30-day money-back guarantee applies to the first 30 days of service on
  any tier

Key Principle

Every cancellation represents a learning opportunity. By handling
departures with care and respect, we preserve relationships and maintain
our reputation. A former client who leaves on good terms may return or
become a valuable referral source.

SECTION 2: CANCELLATION REQUEST HANDLING

When a client requests cancellation, follow this process:

1\. Acknowledge the request promptly within one business day.

2\. Ask for the reason and document it for internal review.

3\. If within 30 days: Process the 30-day money-back guarantee (full
refund, no questions asked).

4\. If after 30 days: Confirm cancellation will take effect at end of
current billing cycle.

5\. Do not pressure the client to stay. If they have concerns we can
address, offer solutions, but respect their decision.

6\. Document the cancellation request in the CRM with a note on the
contact record.

SECTION 3: DATA EXPORT PROCESS

The client owns all their contacts and data. Here is how to export it:

Export Steps

1\. Switch to the client\'s sub-account in GHL.

2\. Go to Contacts.

3\. Select All Contacts (or filter as needed).

4\. Click Export \> CSV.

5\. The export includes: name, email, phone, tags, custom field values,
notes.

6\. Review the export file to ensure completeness.

7\. Send the CSV file to the client via email with a brief explanation
of the columns.

8\. Offer to answer any questions about the data.

What the Client Keeps

- All contact data (exported as CSV)

- All website content they provided (text, images, copy)

- Any reports we generated during their service

What Stays With Us

- CRM pipelines and automation configurations (tied to the GHL platform)

- Website template license (included in the monthly service, not a
  separate purchase)

- Dashboard configurations

- Workflow setups

SECTION 4: WEBSITE TRANSITION (BUILD AND COMMAND TIERS)

If the client had a website with us, follow this transition process:

1\. The client owns their content (text, images, copy they provided).

2\. The template license is part of the monthly service and does not
transfer.

3\. Offer to provide a full text export of all website pages.

4\. If the client has their own hosting, offer to help point their
domain to their new host.

5\. Our hosting and SSL will be deactivated after the billing cycle
ends.

6\. Give the client at least 14 days notice before the website goes
offline.

7\. Recommend they have a new website ready before cancellation takes
effect.

SECTION 5: GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE TRANSITION (COMMAND TIER)

If the client had Local Presence Management with us:

1\. The client retains full ownership of their Google Business Profile
(it is their Google account).

2\. Remove our admin access from their GBP.

3\. Provide a summary of work done: citation list, GMB post history,
review response log.

4\. Advise the client to continue managing their GBP or find an
alternative provider.

5\. Provide a final local presence report as a parting deliverable.

SECTION 6: 30-DAY MONEY-BACK GUARANTEE PROCESS

When a client qualifies for the guarantee (within first 30 days):

1\. Verify the signup date and confirm they are within the 30-day
window.

2\. Process a full refund of their first payment (no partial refunds, no
prorating).

3\. Complete the standard data export (Section 3).

4\. Complete any applicable transitions (Sections 4 and 5).

5\. Send a confirmation email acknowledging the refund and wishing them
well.

6\. Document in CRM: add tag \"cancelled-refund\", add note with reason
and date.

SECTION 7: INTERNAL DOCUMENTATION

For every cancellation, document the following in the CRM contact
record:

1\. Client name and sub-account

2\. Tier they were on

3\. Date of cancellation request

4\. Reason for cancellation (in their words)

5\. Whether 30-day guarantee was invoked

6\. Data export completed (date)

7\. Website transition completed (date, if applicable)

8\. GBP transition completed (date, if applicable)

9\. Final billing date

10\. Any follow-up actions needed

Add a tag to the contact record: \"cancelled\" (or \"cancelled-refund\"
if the guarantee was used).

SECTION 8: WIN-BACK OPPORTUNITIES

Not every cancellation is permanent. After 90 days, consider the
following approach:

1\. Send a friendly check-in email (not a sales pitch).

2\. Ask how things are going and if they found a solution for their
digital presence.

3\. If they express interest, offer a consultation to discuss returning.

4\. If they return, deploy a fresh snapshot (do not reuse the old
sub-account).

5\. Track win-back attempts and success rate.

Win-back outreach demonstrates our commitment to client success and may
recover valuable revenue from former customers.

SECTION 9: CANCELLATION REASON TRACKING

Keep a running log of cancellation reasons to identify patterns:

- Budget/cost concerns

- Not seeing results (clarify timeline expectations during onboarding)

- Found another provider

- Closing their agency

- Switching to in-house management

- Dissatisfied with service quality

- Other

Review this log quarterly to identify areas for improvement. Patterns in
cancellation reasons can reveal gaps in onboarding, communication,
expectation-setting, or product value. Use these insights to strengthen
our service delivery and reduce future churn.
