WHAT IT IS

A2P 10DLC in Plain English

A carrier standard that governs every business text sent to a US consumer over a 10 digit number. Every brand and every campaign must be registered before carriers will reliably deliver the SMS.

Brand Registration

Your agency legal name, EIN, address, website, and vertical get registered with The Campaign Registry (TCR). One time setup per agency.

Campaign Registration

Each message type (lead follow up, appointment reminders, customer service, marketing) is a separate campaign with sample content and opt in language.

Carrier Vetting

The major carriers (T-Mobile, AT&T, Verizon) each review and assign a trust score that controls your daily send throughput.

WHY CARRIERS FILTER

The Enforcement Timeline

Carriers have been tightening the rules since 2022. In 2026 there is no grace period left.

EraWhat ChangedImpact on Agents
Pre 2022Long codes mostly unregulatedHigh delivery, high spam, easy abuse
2022-202310DLC registration introduced, soft enforcementUnregistered traffic still delivered with warnings
2024-2025Hard filtering, surcharges for unregisteredDelivery drops 40 to 70 percent without registration
2026Registration is the default. Unregistered is blocked.No registration means no SMS deliverability, period

COMPLIANT OPT IN LANGUAGE

Sample Consent Templates

Carriers require explicit opt in disclosure wherever you collect a phone number. These are ALE default templates, adaptable to your agency voice.

Web Form Disclosure

"By providing your phone number you agree to receive SMS from [Agency Name] about your insurance quote, appointments, and account updates. Message frequency varies. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help. See our privacy policy and terms."

Checkbox Consent

"I agree to receive transactional and marketing SMS from [Agency Name]. Consent is not a condition of purchase. Reply STOP to cancel."

Every automated message includes agency identification and STOP language. Consent records are stored for 5 years per TCPA retention rules.
