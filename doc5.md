Section 1: Pre-Call Preparation

Research the Prospect Before the Call

Every consultation should be preceded by thorough research. Invest 10-15
minutes before each call to gather intelligence on the prospect agency.
This preparation positions you as knowledgeable and demonstrates genuine
interest in their business.

Key Research Areas:

- Website Review: Assess the quality, mobile responsiveness, and how
  recently it was updated. Look for outdated information, broken links,
  or missing contact information.

- Google Business Profile: Check if their profile is claimed and
  complete. Review star ratings, recent reviews, response rate to
  reviews, and if photos are current.

- Customer Reviews: Read recent reviews on Google, Yelp, and
  industry-specific sites. Note common feedback themes, response
  quality, and sentiment.

- NAP Consistency: Verify Name, Address, and Phone number consistency
  across Google, website, local directories, and Yelp. Inconsistencies
  hurt local search rankings.

- Social Media Presence: Check Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram for
  activity level, posting frequency, and engagement. Inactive accounts
  suggest limited marketing focus.

- CRM Signals: Look for evidence of CRM usage. Signs include integrated
  forms, chatbots on website, email signup boxes, or lead management
  features.

Red Flags to Identify

- Outdated Website: Site hasn\'t been updated in 2+ years, outdated
  design, poor mobile experience, or broken features.

- No Google Reviews: Zero or very few reviews, or none in the past 6
  months. Suggests no active lead generation or customer outreach.

- Inconsistent NAP: Phone number, address, or name varies across
  platforms. Indicates poor digital management.

- No CRM Indicators: Website contact forms go nowhere, no automation, no
  email signup mechanism.

- Negative Reviews: Consistent complaints about responsiveness,
  unprofessional service, or communication delays.

Prospect Qualification Criteria

Before the call, verify that the prospect meets these qualification
criteria:

- Independent Insurance Agency: They own or manage an independent agency
  (not captive). They handle their own marketing decisions.

- Agency Size: 1-10 agents (ideal for our services). Larger agencies may
  have in-house teams.

- Digital Readiness: They express concern about digital presence or have
  pain points we can solve.

- Budget Awareness: They have budget available or can be convinced of
  ROI (compare to lost leads and DIY time).

- Decision Authority: The person you are speaking with can make or
  influence the decision to purchase.

Section 2: The Consultation Call Structure

The consultation call should follow a structured 35-minute framework.
This script is a guided structure, not word-for-word dialogue. Adapt
your language to your style while hitting these key points.

Step 1: Opening (2 minutes)

Objective: Establish rapport, introduce yourself professionally, set
clear expectations for the call, and build trust.

Framework:

- Warm greeting with the prospect\'s name.

- Brief introduction of yourself and Agent Lead Engine.

- Thank them for their time and mention why you\'re calling (we help
  independent agencies generate leads and manage their digital
  presence).

- Set the agenda: \"I\'d like to learn about your agency, understand
  your current challenges, and see if we\'re a good fit. This should
  take about 30 minutes. Does that work for you?\"

- Personal touch: Reference something from your research (e.g., \"I saw
  your recent reviews on Google\" or \"Your website has some great
  content about your specialty lines\").

Step 2: Discovery (10 minutes)

Objective: Understand their business, current systems, pain points, and
priorities. Listen more than you talk.

Key Discovery Questions:

- Tell me about your agency. How long have you been in business, and
  what lines of insurance do you focus on?

- How many agents or staff members do you have, and how is your team
  structured?

- How are you currently capturing leads that come in through your
  website or phone?

- What happens when you miss a call during business hours? How do you
  handle voicemails or after-hours inquiries?

- How do you follow up with prospects who requested a quote but haven\'t
  responded?

- When was the last time your website was updated or redesigned?

- Are you actively managing your Google Business Profile? How often do
  you post or respond to reviews?

- Are you using a CRM system to manage clients and prospects? If so,
  which one?

- How much time per week do you spend on marketing tasks, and how
  satisfied are you with your current marketing results?

Pro Tip: Take notes. Show active listening by referencing their answers
later. Ask follow-up questions like \"Tell me more about that\" or \"How
is that impacting your business?\"

Step 3: Gap Identification (5 minutes)

Objective: Summarize what you heard, validate their challenges, and
identify the specific gaps Agent Lead Engine solves.

Framework:

- Recap: \"Based on what you\'ve shared, I\'m hearing that you
  \[summarize 2-3 pain points\]. Is that accurate?\"

- Validate: \"Many agencies we work with face the same challenges.
  You\'re not alone in this.\"

- Identify Three Core Gaps: We specialize in solving three specific
  gaps. First, lead capture and CRM: automating how leads are captured
  and followed up on. Second, online presence: a professional website
  and Google visibility. Third, local authority: building reviews,
  managing your profile, and dominating local search results.

Step 4: Solution Presentation (10 minutes)

Objective: Present the recommended tier, explain what\'s included, and
show pricing in a way that emphasizes value.

Framework:

- Based on your needs, I recommend \[Tier Name\]. Here\'s what\'s
  included.

- Walk through each tier component: CRM setup, website, local presence
  management, support, reporting.

- Show the investment: \"The investment is \[price\] per month. In
  exchange, you get \[list benefits\]. That works out to \[price
  breakdown\].\"

- Frame value: \"To do this yourself would cost you \$6K to \$9K per
  month in lost productivity and DIY tools. Most agencies pay \$2K to
  \$5K per month to generic agencies without the insurance focus.\"

- Emphasize guarantees: \"30-day money-back guarantee, month-to-month
  commitment, zero long-term contracts.\"

Step 5: Objection Handling (5 minutes)

Objective: Address concerns with empathy and prepared responses. (See
Section 4 for detailed objection scripts.)

Listen first without interrupting. Acknowledge the concern. Ask
clarifying questions. Then respond with a prepared script.

Step 6: Close (3 minutes)

Objective: Move to the next step or get a commitment. Use one of the
closing techniques from Section 5.

- If positive: \"I\'ll send you a proposal summary within an hour. When
  would you like to get started?\" or \"Let me set up your account now.
  Here\'s what happens next.\"

- If hesitant: \"I totally understand you want to think about it. I\'ll
  send you everything in writing. How about we reconnect on \[specific
  date\]?\"

- If still uncertain: \"No pressure at all. I\'ll follow up with you in
  a few days. In the meantime, take a look at the comparison chart I\'m
  sending, and let me know if any questions come up.\"

Section 4: Objection Handling Scripts

Objections are not rejection. They are requests for more information or
reassurance. Use these frameworks to address the most common objections.

Objection 1: That\'s Too Expensive

Response Framework:

- Acknowledge: \"I hear you. Let me put this in perspective.\"

- Compare to DIY: \"If you tried to do this yourself, you\'d spend \$6K
  to \$9K per month in your time alone. You\'d need to hire someone
  part-time, purchase multiple tools, and manage everything
  internally.\"

- Compare to generic agencies: \"Hiring a generic marketing agency costs
  \$2K to \$5K per month, and they don\'t understand insurance. You\'d
  be paying for cookie-cutter strategies.\"

- Value prop: \"We\'re done-for-you, insurance-focused, and include a
  30-day money-back guarantee. You\'re paying for expertise that
  generates leads and converts them.\"

- ROI example: \"If you capture just 5 new clients from our system at an
  average premium of \$2K per year, that pays for months of the
  service.\"

Objection 2: I Can Do This Myself

Response Framework:

- Acknowledge: \"You\'re right, you could. Many agency owners think the
  same thing.\"

- Reality check: \"The challenge is that this requires 10 to 15 hours
  per week. You\'d need to learn web design, CRM management, Google
  Business Profile optimization, and local SEO best practices.\"

- Ask the right question: \"Where are you getting those 10 to 15 hours
  per week right now? Are you taking that time away from client service
  or personal time?\"

- Opportunity cost: \"Your hourly value as an insurance agent is
  probably \$100 to \$200 per hour. That means 10 hours per week is \$1K
  to \$2K in lost revenue or profit.\"

- The real value: \"We take this work completely off your plate. You
  focus on what you\'re best at: selling insurance and serving
  clients.\"

Objection 3: I Already Have a Website

Response Framework:

- Acknowledge: \"Great, that\'s a good start. Let me ask you a few
  questions.\"

- Ask qualifying questions: \"When someone fills out a form on your
  website, where does that lead go? Do you have a CRM capturing those
  inquiries automatically? Are you getting contacted by leads from your
  site regularly?\"

- Dig deeper: \"Is your website mobile-responsive? How fast does it
  load? Are you showing up in Google local search results when someone
  searches for insurance in your area?\"

- Reframe: \"The question isn\'t whether you have a website. It\'s
  whether your website is generating leads and converting them to
  clients. Most agency websites don\'t. We specialize in making sure
  yours does.\"

- Offer integration: \"If you love your current site, we can integrate
  it with our CRM and lead management system. If it needs an upgrade, we
  can rebuild it while keeping your domain and SEO equity.\"

Objection 4: I Need to Think About It

Response Framework:

- Respect the decision: \"That\'s totally fair. This is an important
  decision for your business.\"

- Ask qualifying questions: \"Is there something specific you want to
  think about? Is it the investment, the scope of work, or something
  else?\"

- Send a summary: \"I\'ll send you a detailed proposal summary with the
  tier recommendation, what\'s included, pricing, and our guarantees.
  That way you have everything in writing.\"

- Set a timeline: \"How about we reconnect on \[specific day and time,
  2-3 days out\]? That gives you time to review everything and ask any
  questions.\"

- Reinforce guarantee: \"Remember, there\'s zero risk. You have a 30-day
  money-back guarantee and month-to-month flexibility.\"

Objection 5: I\'m Locked Into Another Vendor Contract

Response Framework:

- Acknowledge: \"That\'s common. When does your contract end?\"

- Plan ahead: \"Let\'s start planning for the transition now. That way,
  when your contract ends, you can move to Agent Lead Engine seamlessly
  without missing a beat.\"

- Add value: \"In the meantime, we can help you plan your website
  migration, CRM setup, and review strategy. When you\'re ready,
  everything will be ready to go.\"

- Offer early enrollment: \"Some clients choose to start with Launch now
  for lead capture while staying with their current vendor for the
  website. Then when that contract ends, you transition everything to
  us.\"

Objection 6: How Do I Know This Will Work?

Response Framework:

- Emphasize guarantees: \"Three guarantees remove the risk. First,
  30-day money-back guarantee with zero questions asked. Second,
  month-to-month, no long-term contracts. Third, on Command tier, we
  offer a 20-lead guarantee.\"

- Share success stories: \"We work with agencies across \[your region\].
  Most see lead volume increase within 30 to 60 days. Here are a few
  examples of what clients experienced.\"

- Show tracking: \"You\'ll see everything in your dashboard. We provide
  monthly reports on leads captured, lead source, conversion rates, and
  ROI. Full transparency.\"

- First-month win: \"In month one, we focus on getting your systems set
  up and starting to capture leads. By month two, you\'ll have data
  showing what\'s working.\"

Objection 7: What Makes You Different From Other Marketing Companies?

Response Framework:

- Insurance focus: \"We work exclusively with insurance agencies. We
  understand your industry, your challenges, and what your ideal client
  looks like. Generic marketing companies don\'t have that depth.\"

- Done-for-you: \"We don\'t hand you a toolkit and ask you to implement
  it. We handle everything. Your job is to close deals and serve
  clients.\"

- Tiered pricing: \"We offer three tiers so you can start small and

  you are in your journey.\"

- Guarantees: \"30-day money-back, 20-lead guarantee on Command,

  contracts upfront.\"

- Integrated solution: \"We\'re not just a website company or a CRM
  company. We handle everything: website, CRM, local search, review
  management, lead capture, follow-up, and reporting. One partner,
  complete system.\"

Section 5: Closing Techniques

The close should feel natural, not pushy. Choose a technique based on
the prospect\'s energy and readiness level.

Technique 1: The Assumptive Close

Use when: The prospect has shown strong buying signals and is engaged.

Example script: \"Based on what you\'ve told me about your needs and
your agency size, Build is the right fit. Let me get you started. I\'ll
send you the onboarding checklist, and we\'ll set up your account by
\[specific date\].\"

Technique 2: The Timeline Close

Use when: The prospect is hesitant or slow to decide. Urgency helps.

Example script: \"Every day without a system is a day you lose leads
that could be coming to your website right now. When would you like to
go live? We can have you set up within a week if we start today.\"

Technique 3: The Guarantee Close

Use when: The prospect is concerned about risk or commitment.

Example script: \"There\'s zero risk. You have a 30-day money-back
guarantee, no long-term contract, and month-to-month flexibility. If
it\'s not right for you, we\'ll refund everything. But I\'m confident
you\'ll see results within the first month.\"

Technique 4: The Comparison Close

Use when: The prospect is comparing options or unsure about value.

Example script: \"Let me show you this comparison. If you do this
yourself, you\'re looking at \$6K to \$9K per month in lost time. A
generic agency charges \$2K to \$5K per month without insurance
expertise. We charge \[price\] and we\'re 100% focused on insurance
agencies. You get specialty service at a fair price.\"

Technique 5: The Soft Close

Use when: The prospect needs more time or is genuinely uncertain.

Example script: \"I completely respect that you want to take time with
this decision. Here\'s what I\'d like to do. I\'ll send you a one-page
summary with the tier recommendation, pricing, and next steps. Review it
when you have time. Then let\'s set up a follow-up call for \[specific
date\]. If you have questions in the meantime, just reach out.\"

Section 6: Post-Call Follow-Up

The follow-up process is where deals close. Most sales happen in the
second, third, or fourth touch after the initial call. Be persistent,
but respectful.

Step 1: Send Recap Email Within 1 Hour

Subject line: \"Quick recap of our call and next steps\"

Recap email should include:

- Personal greeting and thank you for the time.

- Summary of what you discussed (their pain points and goals).

- Tier recommendation and pricing breakdown.

- What\'s included in the recommended tier (with link to details page).

- Guarantees and terms (30-day money-back, month-to-month, no
  contracts).

- Next step and timeline (when to expect onboarding if they say yes).

- Call to action with clear next step (reply to this email, click link
  to start, etc.).

Step 2: Follow-Up Sequence (If No Response)

- Day 0: Send recap email (within 1 hour of call).

- Day 2: Follow-up email. Subject: \"Quick question about \[agency
  name\]\". Keep it short and ask if they had a chance to review the
  proposal.

- Day 5: Phone call. Keep it brief. Say: \"I wanted to check in. Did you
  get a chance to review the proposal? Any questions I can answer?\"

- Day 10: Final email. Subject: \"Last check-in on your lead generation
  plan\". Let them know you\'re here to help and offer to set up a brief
  call to answer any remaining questions.

- After Day 10: Move to nurture list. Send occasional content and check
  in quarterly.

Tracking in Your CRM

Use your CRM to track:

- Lead source: Where did they come from (inbound, referral, paid, etc.)?

- Consultation date: When you had the call.

- Tier recommended: Launch, Build, or Command?

- Prospect stage: Interested, considering, objection, or closed.

- Follow-up tasks: Auto-schedule tasks for 2-day and 5-day follow-ups.

- Close date: When they signed (if applicable).

- Contract value: Monthly MRR of the deal.

Monthly Review Process

At the end of each month, review:

- Total consultations held and close rate percentage.

- MRR generated and average deal size.

- Tier distribution: How many Launch, Build, and Command deals?

- Time to close: Average number of days from consultation to signed
  deal.

- Most common objections: Identify patterns and refine your responses.

- Pipeline value: Total MRR of prospects in the pipeline by stage.

Continuous Improvement

Use your metrics to identify areas for improvement:

- Low close rate: Review call recordings, refine your discovery
  questions, improve objection handling.

- Low average deal size: Train on tier recommendation logic and value
  prop for higher tiers.

- Slow close time: Improve follow-up sequences, increase call frequency,
  refine urgency messaging.

- Common objections: Role-play new responses, practice your value prop
  with that specific concern.

- Weak pipeline: Increase lead generation efforts, expand cold outreach,
  or refine qualification criteria.

Annual Sales Goals Example

If your target is 10 new customers per month:

- At a 30% close rate, you need 33 consultations per month.

- At an average deal size of \$950/mo, that\'s \$9,500 MRR per month.

- Annually: 120 new customers, \$114,000 annual revenue (at year one
  rates).

Track these metrics monthly to stay on pace.

Final Notes

Sales is a numbers game. The more consultations you have, the more deals
you close. But quality matters. A well-researched consultation with a
qualified prospect and a strong pitch will close at much higher rates
than rushing through unqualified leads.

Focus on:

- Quality consultations (prepare beforehand)

- Strong value prop (insurance-focused, done-for-you, guarantees)

- Consistent follow-up (the second and third touches close most deals)

- Continuous improvement (track metrics, refine messaging, practice
  objection handling)

You\'ve got this. Go close some deals.
