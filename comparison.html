DIY vs. Generic Agency vs. Agent Lead Engine

There are three realistic ways to build the digital presence an insurance agency needs. Here is what each one actually costs, how long it takes, and what you give up.

THREE PATHS

Your Options (and What Each One Costs)

All three can work. They do not cost the same, take the same time, or deliver the same result.

DIY Stack

$6K-$9K/mo effective

You buy the tools and run them yourself. Low hard cost, huge time cost.

What You Pay:

  • CRM subscription, SMS, email, calendar
  • Website builder and hosting
  • GMB and citation tools
  • 10 to 15 hours a week of your time
  • Lost revenue on missed leads while you learn
  • No compliance safety net

Generic Agency

$2K-$5K/mo

A marketing agency that serves everyone: dentists, lawyers, plumbers, and you.

What You Pay:

  • High monthly retainer
  • Cookie-cutter templates reused across industries
  • Ramp-up time while they learn insurance
  • 12-month contracts common
  • No insurance-specific compliance
  • Generic lead workflows

Done-for-you CRM, website, and local search, built only for insurance.

  • 5 insurance lines pre-configured
  • 9 premium templates, each unique
  • Compliance baked into every workflow
  • $0 setup, no contracts, 30-day money back
  • Launch in 48 hours to 14 days
  • About 1 hour a month of your time

HONEST GUIDANCE

When Each Option Actually Makes Sense

We will tell you the truth: sometimes the answer is not us.

Pick DIY If...

  • You are already a marketing technologist
  • You have 10 to 15 free hours every week
  • You enjoy configuring CRMs and compliance
  • You are fine losing leads during the learning curve

Pick a Generic Agency If...

  • You have a $50K+ annual marketing budget
  • You need a specialist in paid ads at scale
  • You are fine teaching them insurance
  • Long contracts do not worry you

Pick Agent Lead Engine If...

  • You want to sell insurance, not run marketing
  • You want insurance-specific workflows on day one
  • You need launch in days, not months
  • You want a money-back guarantee in writing
